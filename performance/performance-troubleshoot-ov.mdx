---
title: "General troubleshooting approach"
description: "A step-by-step guide to systematically diagnose performance problems in RisingWave."
---

When you encounter performance problems in RisingWave, follow these steps to systematically identify the root cause:

1. **Check overall throughput and latency:** Start by examining the key performance indicators described in [Monitoring and metrics](/performance/metrics). Are your queries exhibiting high latency or low throughput? What are the specific values you're seeing, and how do they compare to your expected performance? High latency can often indicate issues with query structure or resource constraints.

2. **Locate the bottleneck using job/fragment-level metrics:** Open the **Streaming** section of the Grafana dashboard (dev) and consult the **bottleneck percentage tables** to identify which streaming job or fragment is responsible for the most resource consumption. Then examine the per-job/fragment **CPU usage**, **memory usage**, and **actor busy-rate** panels to determine whether the hot fragment is CPU-bound, memory-bound, or otherwise constrained. See [Monitoring and metrics - Streaming job and fragment metrics](/performance/metrics#streaming-job-and-fragment-metrics) for details. This direct resource view is typically the fastest way to pinpoint the root cause.

3. **Corroborate with backpressure analysis (if downstream blockage is suspected):** If the bottleneck tables and resource panels do not clearly reveal a saturated resource, or if you suspect that a downstream component is blocking upstream progress (graph-propagation effect), check the **Actor Output Blocking Time Ratio (Backpressure)** panel under **Streaming Actors**. Backpressure propagates upstream, so finding the *frontmost* back-pressured fragment helps confirm where the actual bottleneck lies. See [Workload analysis](/performance/workload-analysis) and [Troubleshooting high latency](/performance/troubleshoot-high-latency) for detailed steps.

4. **Examine resource utilization in depth:** For confirmed resource constraints, use [Monitoring and metrics](/performance/metrics) and [Specific bottlenecks](/performance/specific-bottlenecks) to drill into CPU, memory, cache, storage, or compaction metrics and identify the precise cause.

5. **Check for errors:** Examine RisingWave's logs for any error messages that might indicate the source of the problem.

6. **Simplify the query:** Try to remove parts of the SQL query to isolate which part is causing the issues.

By following these steps, and using the detailed guidance in the linked sections, you can effectively diagnose and resolve most performance problems in RisingWave.
